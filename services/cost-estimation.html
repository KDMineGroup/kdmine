Our Approach

Cost Estimation Methodology

We combine four complementary pricing methodologies to maximise accuracy at each AACE estimate class — from factored screening to vendor-quoted definitive estimates.

1
📚

Factored / Analogous

Scale costs from analogous projects using capacity-based scaling factors (0.6 exponent rule). Used for Class 4–5 estimates. KDMine database: 200+ analogous projects.

2
🗃️

Equipment-Based (CostMine)

Price individual equipment items from CostMine 2026 database (850+ items) with installation factors, freight, and commissioning allowances. Primary method for Class 3–4.

3
📩

Vendor Budgetary Quotes

Obtain budgetary quotes from OEM vendors for major equipment (SAG mills, HPGR, autoclaves, large pumps). Required for Class 2–3. KDMine maintains active vendor relationships globally.

4
🎲

Monte Carlo Risk Analysis

10,000-iteration Monte Carlo simulation with PERT/triangular distributions and correlation matrix to generate P50/P80/P90 contingency outputs. Replaces fixed-% contingency for Class 3+.

Common Questions

CAPEX/OPEX Estimation FAQ

What is the typical accuracy of a Class 3 CAPEX estimate?
+
AACE 47R-11 defines Class 3 accuracy as −10% to +20% at the P50 level. In practice, KDMine targets −15% to +20% for Class 3 mining estimates. The asymmetry (wider upside range) reflects the reality that mining projects more commonly overrun than underrun. Monte Carlo analysis is used to quantify the P80 and P90 contingency requirements rather than applying a fixed percentage.
How do you handle equipment pricing for remote or sanctioned jurisdictions?
+
For projects in Iran and other jurisdictions with import restrictions, KDMine applies a three-layer pricing approach: (1) Identify locally manufactured equivalents where available, (2) Apply import duty, freight, and insurance premiums (typically +15–35% on FOB price), (3) Add lead time risk premium to contingency for long-lead items. We maintain a database of Iranian and regional equipment manufacturers and their 2026 pricing for common mining equipment categories.
What contingency percentage should I use for a Class 3 estimate?
+
KDMine does not recommend using a fixed contingency percentage for Class 3 estimates. Instead, we use Monte Carlo simulation to derive risk-based contingency. Typical results for open pit projects: P50 contingency 8–12%, P80 contingency 15–20%, P90 contingency 22–28%. Complex projects (HPAL, refractory gold, underground) typically require 5–8% higher contingency at each percentile.
Can you update an existing CAPEX estimate to 2026 pricing?
+
Yes. CAPEX estimate updates (re-basing) are one of our most common engagements. We apply category-specific escalation indices to update each cost line item from its original pricing date to June 2026. Typical re-basing engagement: 3–6 weeks, depending on estimate complexity. We also identify line items that require re-pricing from first principles.
