Our Methodology

How We Deliver a Bankable Feasibility Study

Our 8-phase feasibility methodology is designed to produce a study that withstands lender due diligence, regulatory review, and independent technical audit.

1
Scope Definition & BoE
Basis of Estimate Document
Define study scope, inclusions/exclusions, pricing basis, and AACE class. Establish the Basis of Estimate (BoE) document — the contractual foundation of the study.
2–3 weeks
2
Geology & Resource Model
NI 43-101 / JORC Compliant
Review and validate the geological resource model. Confirm resource/reserve classification under NI 43-101 or JORC. Establish the LOM production schedule basis.
4–6 weeks
3
Mining Engineering & LOM Schedule
Pit Design, Fleet Sizing, Schedule
Optimised pit design (Whittle/Pseudoflow), mine scheduling (LOM), haul truck and drill fleet sizing, waste dump design, and geotechnical slope analysis.
6–10 weeks
4
Metallurgy & Process Design
Flowsheet, Mass Balance, Equipment Sizing
Process flowsheet development, mass balance, equipment sizing (SAG/ball mills, flotation, leach, SX-EW), reagent consumption, and water balance.
8–12 weeks
5
Infrastructure & Site Services
Power, Water, Access, Camp, Tailings
Site infrastructure design: power supply, water supply and treatment, access roads, camp facilities, and tailings storage facility (TSF) design.
6–8 weeks
6
CAPEX / OPEX Estimation
AACE 47R-11 Compliant | CostMine 2025
Full CAPEX estimate (direct, indirect, owner's costs, contingency) and LOM OPEX model using CostMine 2025 pricing. Monte Carlo contingency analysis with P50/P80/P90 outputs.
4–6 weeks
7
Financial Modelling & Economics
DCF / NPV / IRR / Sensitivity
Full DCF financial model: LOM cash flow, NPV/IRR, payback period, sensitivity analysis (commodity price, CAPEX, OPEX, discount rate), and project finance structuring.
3–4 weeks
8
Report, Review & ITA Preparation
Independent Technical Audit Ready
Compile the full feasibility report, conduct internal peer review, address QP/CP sign-off requirements, and prepare the study package for Independent Technical Audit (ITA) by lender-appointed engineers.
4–6 weeks

Common Questions

Feasibility Study FAQ

How long does a Class 3 bankable feasibility study take?
+
A Class 3 bankable feasibility study for a greenfield open pit project typically takes 9–14 months from scope definition to final report. Underground projects and complex metallurgy (HPAL, refractory gold) typically require 12–18 months. The critical path is usually metallurgical testwork (6–9 months) and geotechnical investigation (4–6 months) — both of which should begin before the formal feasibility study commences.
What is the difference between a pre-feasibility and a bankable feasibility study?
+
A pre-feasibility study (AACE Class 4, ±30%) is used to select the preferred development option and confirm the project is worth advancing. A bankable feasibility study (AACE Class 3, ±20%) is the minimum standard required for institutional project financing, NI 43-101/JORC reserve declaration, and construction decision. The key differences are: engineering completion (1–15% vs. 10–40%), pricing basis (factored vs. vendor quotes), and contingency methodology (fixed % vs. Monte Carlo).
How much does a Class 3 feasibility study cost?
+
The cost of a Class 3 feasibility study is typically 0.5–1.5% of the project's estimated CAPEX. For a $500M project, this means $2.5–7.5M. The wide range reflects project complexity: a simple heap leach operation costs less than a complex HPAL plant. KDMine provides fixed-price proposals with clearly defined scope — no scope creep surprises. Contact us for a project-specific proposal within 5 business days.
Can KDMine provide a Qualified Person (QP) or Competent Person (CP) sign-off?
+
Yes. KDMine has registered QPs under NI 43-101 (Canada) and CPs under JORC (Australia/international) on staff. Our QPs and CPs are members of recognised professional organisations including APEGBC, AusIMM, and SME. We can provide QP/CP sign-off for all technical sections of the feasibility study, including geology, mining, processing, and economics.
What data do you need from us to start a feasibility study?
+
Minimum data requirements for a Class 3 feasibility study: (1) Geological resource model with supporting drill data, (2) Metallurgical testwork results (minimum 500kg composite samples), (3) Geotechnical investigation data, (4) Site survey and topographic data, (5) Environmental baseline data (12 months minimum). We can advise on data gaps and help design additional investigation programs.
Do you work on projects in Iran and Central Asia?
+
Yes. Iran and Central Asia are core markets for KDMine Group. We have completed 18 feasibility studies in Iran (copper, iron ore, zinc, lead, gold) and 8 in Central Asia (Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan). Our team includes Persian and Russian-speaking engineers with deep knowledge of local regulatory frameworks, equipment import logistics, labour markets, and energy costs.
